# 100 Free Resource Websites That Should Be Illegal

A curated list of genuinely useful free tools, organized by category. Every link below has been checked and points to the real, authentic site. Honest one-line reviews included — freemium tools and legally grey sites are flagged so you know exactly what you're getting.

---

## Media, Streaming & Audio

1. **[cobalt.tools](https://cobalt.tools/)** — Download video/audio from almost any social platform. Clean, no ads, no watermarks. Genuinely the best in its class.
2. **[justwatch.com](https://www.justwatch.com/)** — Tells you which streaming service has the movie/show you want. Saves you hopping between apps. Totally free.
3. **[archive.org](https://archive.org/)** — The Internet Archive: old web pages, plus free public-domain movies, music, books and software. A civilizational treasure.
4. **[archive.ph](https://archive.ph/)** — Permanently snapshot any web page. Handy for citations and dead-link insurance (also bypasses some paywalls — use responsibly).
5. **[tunefind.com](https://www.tunefind.com/)** — Find the exact song from any TV/movie scene. Crowd-sourced and reliable, though the UI is ad-heavy.
6. **[radio.garden](https://radio.garden/)** — Spin a globe and listen to live radio from anywhere on Earth. Oddly addictive, completely free.
7. **[musicforprogramming.net](https://musicforprogramming.net/)** — Long-form ambient mixes built for deep focus. No account, no fluff.
8. **[mynoise.net](https://mynoise.net/)** — Customizable noise generators (rain, cafes, etc.) with per-frequency sliders. Donation-supported but fully usable free.
9. **[summarize.tech](https://www.summarize.tech/)** — AI summaries of long YouTube videos. Great for lectures/podcasts; free tier covers most needs.
10. **[subtitlecat.com](https://subtitlecat.com/)** — Auto-generates and translates subtitles for videos. Quality varies, but it's free and fast.
11. **[audiomass.co](https://audiomass.co/)** — A surprisingly capable audio editor that runs entirely in your browser. No install, no signup.
12. **[clipchamp.com](https://clipchamp.com/en/)** — Browser video editor (now owned by Microsoft). Solid free tier; some export options and stock assets are paid.

## Image, Photo & Design Tools

13. **[photopea.com](https://www.photopea.com/)** — A full Photoshop clone in your browser. Opens PSD files. Ad-supported but free and astonishingly powerful.
14. **[remove.bg](https://www.remove.bg/)** — One-click background removal. Free previews; full-resolution HD downloads cost credits.
15. **[cleanup.pictures](https://cleanup.pictures/)** — Erase objects/people from photos. Free at standard resolution; HD is paid.
16. **[unscreen.com](https://www.unscreen.com/)** — Remove backgrounds from video clips. Free tier is low-res/watermarked but useful for quick jobs.
17. **[squoosh.app](https://squoosh.app/)** — Google's image compressor with live before/after comparison. Free, open-source, works offline.
18. **[tinypng.com](https://tinypng.com/)** — Dead-simple PNG/JPEG/WebP compression that "just works." Free for everyday use.
19. **[tineye.com](https://tineye.com/)** — Reverse image search that's often better than Google for finding where an image originated. Free for basic searches.
20. **[undraw.co](https://undraw.co/)** — Open-source illustrations you can recolor to match your brand. No attribution required.
21. **[heroicons.com](https://heroicons.com/)** — Clean, MIT-licensed SVG icons from the Tailwind team. Free for any use.
22. **[lucide.dev](https://lucide.dev/)** — A large, consistent open-source icon set (fork of Feather). Free, well-maintained.
23. **[coolors.co](https://coolors.co/)** — Generate color palettes in seconds with the spacebar. Free tier is generous.

## Free Stock Photos & Video

24. **[unsplash.com](https://unsplash.com/)** — High-resolution stock photos, free for commercial use. The gold standard.
25. **[pexels.com](https://www.pexels.com/)** — Free stock photos *and* video. Strong selection, no attribution required.
26. **[pixabay.com](https://pixabay.com/)** — Free images, vectors, illustrations and music. Quality is mixed but the catalog is huge.

## PDF & Document Tools

27. **[tinywow.com](https://tinywow.com/)** — 100+ free PDF/image/writing tools in one place. No signup; files auto-delete.
28. **[smallpdf.com](https://smallpdf.com/)** — Polished PDF toolkit. Free tier limits you to a couple of tasks per day.
29. **[ilovepdf.com](https://www.ilovepdf.com/)** — Merge, split, compress, convert PDFs. Reliable and free for most tasks.
30. **[pdf24.org](https://www.pdf24.org/en/)** — A genuinely full PDF toolkit, free with no real catches. An underrated gem.
31. **[sejda.com](https://www.sejda.com/)** — Browser-based PDF editor that actually lets you edit text. Free with daily limits.

## Free Books & Libraries

32. **[gutenberg.org](https://gutenberg.org/)** — 70,000+ public-domain classics, free and legal. The original free ebook library.
33. **[standardebooks.org](https://standardebooks.org/)** — Public-domain books, lovingly reformatted and typeset. Free and beautiful.
34. **[pdfdrive.com](https://www.pdfdrive.com/)** — Massive free PDF book search. ⚠️ Mixed catalog includes copyrighted titles — legally grey.
35. **[libgen.li](https://libgen.li/)** — Millions of free textbooks and papers. ⚠️ Piracy — legally grey in most countries.
36. **[annas-archive.gs](https://annas-archive.gs/)** — Search engine for essentially every book ever digitized. ⚠️ Piracy — legally grey. (The old `.org` domain is dead; `.gs` is the current mirror.)
